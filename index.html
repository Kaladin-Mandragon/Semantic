King Midas

Who Was He?

King Midas was a king that was favored by the gods and was given a wish by them. He chose to be able to turn anything he touched into gold. BAM! He touched a plate, it is now gold. BAM! He touched a tree, now it is solid gold.

Why Was His Wish Foolish?

After enjoying turning his flowers and plates and silverware into solid gold items, he called to his servants to bring him some food because he was hungry. He was brought an apple on one of his new fancy golden plates. He picked it up to take a bite. BAM! His teeth struck the solid gold apple.

His eyes grew larger as he began to panic. How would he be able to eat! As fear began to set in, he heard the melodious sound of his daughter's laughter as she entered the room. She ran up to him and said "Daddy, come see what I can do outside!"

Before he would warn her or pull his hand away she grabbed his hand and turned to pull him out of his chair and take him outside. Before his very eyes his beautiful daughter turned to gold. As he fell to the ground beside her in grief, he noticed her face that was now etched from gold containing a grin of childlike joy.

Tips For Making A Wish

  1. Be very specific with wishes so that there are fewer ways that things could end badly
    • Make the effect only happen in certain circumstances to avoid accidental effects
    • Set limits upon the wish so that certain things are unable to happen
  2. Think of all possible situations in which your wish would be an issue and think of loopholes to avoid those situations
  3. Avoid wishes that may collapse the economy unless that is actually your final goal

Morals To Be Learned

There are many things that you should take away and implement from the story of King Midas.

To Think
You should think before you make a wish because there may be consequences that you are unable to foresee.
Be Selfless
You should be selfless and not greedy due to issues that may arise from constantly desiring more.
Avoid All Powerful Beings
In avoiding All Powerful Beings you lower the chances of being given a curse or or side affect while making wishes.

Do not follow in the steps of the foolish King Midas
